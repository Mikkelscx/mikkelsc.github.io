Brief

How can Durex help breaking sexual barriers?

Problem

People don't know their way around all the pronouns and the fear of approaching people outside the cis spectrum is combined with fear in lack of confidence.

Audience insights

Both cis and non-binary wants to be polite and not misgender individuals. Unfortunately this tend to scare off suitors, in fear of accidental discrimination.

Solution

Durex launches a 'Non-binary Edition' of the classic boardgames, Guess Who. The purpose of the game is to make people conversate in non-binary terms in a fun, insightful and safe way.

Campaign Video

Instructions

For 2 Players

Object of the game

Guess your opponent's Mystery Person before they guess yours.

Gameplay

Shuffle the mystery cards. Choose one card each at random and fit it into the slot. Ask questions that can only be responded with 'YES or NO'. Continue until you feel confident in guessing your opponent's Mystery Person.

Non-binary rules

In this 'Non-binary version' you can't ask the usual question of the character being either male or female as all of the characters identify as Non-binary. You may only refer to the characters as they/them (e.g. "Do they have a moustache?" and "No, they haven't").

There are no punishments or minus points for misgendering in the game as its purpose and goal is to educate and hopefully gain habits with gender neutral pronouns.

Remember:

In the real world of socialization you don't have to assume individuals as they/them in advance. Simply introduce yourself in your preferred pronouns and ask them what they would like you to refer them as. (e.g. "Hi I'm 'name' and I go by 'preferred pronouns'. How about you?")
